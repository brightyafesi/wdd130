LAKES

These are the five largest lakes known in the world.

Caspian sea

Although it shares the characteristics of a sea, the Caspian sea is the the largest lake known, with an area of approx 371,000 km2 (143,000 mi2).

The Caspian has a a max depth of approx 1,025 m (3,363 ft), a length of approx 1,199 km (745 mi), and a volume of approx 78,200 km3 (18,800 mi3).

It is a saline lake located amidst the shorelines of Kazakhstan, Turkmenistan, Azerbaijan, Russia and Iran.

×

Lake Superior

Lake Superior is the second largest lake known, with an area of approx 82,100 km2 (mi2).

The Superior has a max depth of approx 406 m (1,332 ft), a length of approx 616 km (383 mi), and a volume of approx 82,100 km3 (31,700 mi3).

It is a fresh water lake located amidst the shorelines of Canada and the United States.

Lake Victoria

The Victoria has a max depth of approx 84 m (276 ft), a length of approx 322 km (200 mi), and a volume of approx km3 (mi3).

It is a fresh water lake located amidst the shorelines of Uganda, Kenya and Tanzania

Lake Huron

Lake Huron is the fourth largest lake known, with an area of approx 59,600 km2 (23,000 mi2).

The Huron has a max depth of approx 229 m (751 ft), a length of approx 332 km (206 mi), and a volume of 3,540 km3 (850 mi3).

It is a fresh water lake located amidst the shorelines of Canada and the United States.

Lake Michigan

Lake Michigan is the fifth largest lake known, with an area of approx 58,000 km2 (22,000 mi2).

The Michigan has a max depth of approx 281 m (922 ft), a length of approx 494 km (307 mi), and a volume of approx 4,900 km3 (1,200 mi3).

It is a fresh water lake located in entirely in the United States.
